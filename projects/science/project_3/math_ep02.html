Dot Product (Scalar) i
$\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b} = \|\mathbf{a}\|\|\mathbf{b}\|\cos\theta = \textstyle\sum a_i b_i$

Projects one vector onto another. Zero when perpendicular.

Cross Product (Vector) i
$\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b} = \|\mathbf{a}\|\|\mathbf{b}\|\sin\theta\,\hat{\mathbf{n}}$

Perpendicular to both. Magnitude = area of parallelogram.

Norm & Unit Vector
$\|\mathbf{v}\| = \sqrt{v_1^2 + v_2^2 + \cdots + v_n^2}$
$\hat{\mathbf{v}} = \mathbf{v} / \|\mathbf{v}\|$

Unit vector has length 1 — preserves direction only.

Vector Projection
$\operatorname{proj}_\mathbf{b}\mathbf{a} = \dfrac{\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}}{\|\mathbf{b}\|^2}\,\mathbf{b}$

Shadow of a onto b. Foundation of Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization.

Vector Spaces — The 8 Axioms

A vector space V over ℝ satisfies closure under addition and scalar multiplication, commutativity, associativity, zero vector, additive inverse, and distributive laws. Any set obeying these rules is a vector space — including functions, polynomials, matrices, and audio signals.

V1 u + v ∈ V (closure)
V2 u + v = v + u
V3 (u+v)+w = u+(v+w)
V4 ∃ 0 : u + 0 = u
V5 ∃ −u : u + (−u) = 0
V6 αu ∈ V
V7 α(u+v) = αu + αv
V8 1·u = u

Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors

An eigenvector of A is a special direction that doesn't rotate under the transformation — it only scales. The scaling factor is the eigenvalue. These "characteristic" directions reveal the deep structure of any linear map.

Eigenvector visualizer — stretched, not rotated

Eigenvalue Equation i
$A\mathbf{v} = \lambda\mathbf{v}$

v is only scaled, never rotated. λ is the scale factor.

Characteristic Polynomial
$\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$
For 2×2: $\lambda^2 - \operatorname{tr}(A)\lambda + \det(A) = 0$

Trace = sum of eigenvalues. Det = product of eigenvalues.

Diagonalization
$A = P\,D\,P^{-1}$

P = eigenvectors as columns. D = eigenvalues on diagonal. Decouples the system.

Real-world Applications
PCA — Principal component analysis, data compression
PageRank — Google's web importance ranking
Vibrations — Normal modes of mechanical systems
Quantum — Measurable values are eigenvalues of operators
Stability — |λ| < 1 → stable fixed point in dynamics

Fourier Series i
$f(x) = \dfrac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\!\left[a_n\cos\tfrac{2\pi nx}{L} + b_n\sin\tfrac{2\pi nx}{L}\right]$
Fourier Transform
$\hat{f}(\xi) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x)\,e^{-2\pi i x\xi}\,dx$

Generalizes to non-periodic functions. Gives a continuous frequency spectrum.

Euler's Formula — the bridge
$e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta$

Complex exponentials are sinusoids. This is why Fourier and complex analysis intertwine.

Parseval's Theorem
$\int|f(x)|^2\,dx = \int|\hat{f}(\xi)|^2\,d\xi$

Total energy is preserved. Time domain energy = frequency domain energy.

Separation of Variables
$u(x,t)=X(x)\,T(t)$

Split a PDE into two ODEs. Works when boundary conditions align with coordinate axes.

Laplacian Operator
$\nabla^2 u = \dfrac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} + \dfrac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2}$

Measures how u differs from its local average. Zero = harmonic function.

Green's Functions

Impulse response of a linear operator. The point-source solution encodes everything.
